## Definition

AI-native managing general agents (MGAs) and underwriting platforms
serving specialty insurance niches where (a) the risk is novel or
poorly priced, (b) traditional carriers lack the data infrastructure
to underwrite confidently, and (c) sale-leaseback or other structured
capital flows create distribution leverage. The current focal niche
is hyperscale data-center insurance; adjacent niches include
cyber/specialty-tech and cannabis.

## Why now

- Hyperscale data-center capacity is being deployed at unprecedented
  scale with insurance markets that haven't caught up — incumbents
  (Chubb, AIG, Zurich) are either not active or under-capacity in the
  specialty layer.
- AI underwriting tools materially shift the unit economics of
  novel-risk lines that have historically been uneconomic to write.
- The capital structure (Lloyd's reinsurance behind an MGA, plus
  specialty equity backers) lets a small team write large lines
  without needing balance-sheet capacity.
- Multiple credible operators in the network ([[Mark Watson]],
  [[Brian Earhart]], [[Dan]], [[Dave]]) actively want to build here.

## Investment criteria

What this thesis bets on:
- An MGA structure with a defensible underwriting edge — not "we have
  AI" but a specific data or distribution moat
- A specialty line where traditional capacity is absent or rationed
- An operator with deep insurance experience (Argo, Alliant, Lexington,
  Aon backgrounds)
- A path to Lloyd's reinsurance capacity
- A distribution wedge — sale-leaseback referrals, broker partnerships,
  hyperscaler procurement teams

## What we'd pass on

- "AI underwriting" with no proprietary data moat
- Insurtech distribution plays without an underwriting edge
- Generalist insurance technology — has to be specialty
- Companies whose value proposition depends on incumbent carriers
  changing how they work

## Tension with the vertical-agents pillar

This is **not a vertical AI agents bet** — it's a capital-structured
specialty insurance bet that uses AI as a tool. The cost-of-capital
profile is fundamentally different (slow-money, balance-sheet-adjacent,
infrastructure-shaped). Holding this and the vertical-agents pillar
simultaneously is fine *if* the owner has decided both deserve
attention; not fine if AI-native insurance is just where the
conversations happen to be going. Decide.
