### 2026-05-07 — Confidential PE over-allocation reduction project
<!-- source_hash: 33447a39537a0c984c9df187f8542766c0c043a8733b8537d447529db2e6cc26 -->
Jeremy disclosed, on a confidential basis, that his organization is significantly over-allocated to private equity — nearly twice their target weighting — and is engaged in a large project to reduce that exposure via a managed fund secondary sale, in which fund interests are transferred into a special purpose vehicle and roughly half is sold, preserving GP relationships while reducing the drag on distributions. The over-allocation stemmed from two decisions: acquiring a funeral insurance business with long-dated liabilities that required heavy private equity backing, followed by an M&A team oversight that missed severe customer concentration risk and a contract expiration six months post-close, which resulted in the loss of that contract. A subsequent pivot toward annuities further reduced the attractiveness of private equity as an asset class, and three years of distribution drought left the portfolio badly out of balance.
