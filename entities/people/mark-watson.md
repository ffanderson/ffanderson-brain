### 2026-04-27 — Reinsurance as negative-cost capital
<!-- source_hash: e3b23879b186b228edf12f031ec8dde8dd02c4ad9a3ea83bf487722e66d4d2ef -->
Mark explained that at [[Argo]], he used proportional (quota share) reinsurance treaties to generate what amounted to negative-cost capital: ceding commissions paid by reinsurers exceeded his origination costs by roughly five-plus points (35%+ received vs. ~30% cost), effectively funding underwriting growth with reinsurer money. The model worked because Argo's pricing and distribution consistently produced gross loss ratios better than net, giving reinsurers a decade-long track record of profitability and motivating them to pay above-market commissions.
