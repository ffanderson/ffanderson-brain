### 2026-05-07 — LLMs, Wolfram integration, and boring engineering
<!-- source_hash: c1cbf444f00990cabbd78769085e004dcb463b48400c2320903980ec92011096 -->
Stephen Wolfram told the fellowship cohort that connecting LLMs to computational knowledge systems like Wolfram Alpha has been technically feasible since two weeks after ChatGPT's release, but that the actual technology for making those connections has evolved and much of the work is 'frankly kind of boring' software engineering. He noted that his platform has real computational knowledge valuable as a foundational tool for foundation models, and that getting an LLM to know when to stop calling external tools remains a non-trivial problem.
