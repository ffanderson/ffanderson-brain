### 2026-05-05 — Structural view on DC insurance capital
<!-- source_hash: 2f242f84ac6cc59a5ec2222c115dcfb0580dee6a6d727656fa1e4c64fbcd4b25 -->
Dave argued that the core barrier to insuring hyperscale data centers is not pricing but the lack of clear exposure aggregation, which prevents new capital from entering the market. He noted that the industry can handle campuses up to around $5 billion but has no capital to cope with $20–30 billion hyperscale campuses loaded with chips. He described primary-layer pricing as 'inverted' — an hourglass shape where capacity charges at the top exceed the layers below — and said the balance-sheet insurance business is not an attractive venture opportunity.
