### 2026-04-01 — Life insurer LP base and services
<!-- source_hash: 91eb914e1b6f9f37763810f5c40c953b8ae30449825d1977df98e454426972be -->
The bulk of capital for the Vestigo funds came from life insurance companies — including [[Western & Southern]], [[CUNA Mutual]], [[Guardian]], and [[Equitable]] — who invested as strategic LPs at roughly $10M each. Fraser and his partners serviced these relationships through quarterly half-day strategic meetings, bespoke analytics work (including traffic analysis for the [[Gerber Life]] acquisition by [[Western & Southern]]), and introductions to portfolio company CEOs, with Fraser personally performing much of the data science and ML work.

### 2026-05-05 — Restrictive conflict-of-interest policy
<!-- source_hash: 052bc16ede5aab5729d203b724d1efc3c2c76b12badb7b77628baea68a2f2ee8 -->
Fraser's meeting partner described Vestigo as highly institutional, with strict policies that prohibited angel investing and outside entrepreneurial activity for its junior partners. He viewed this as a structural mistake for retaining ambitious early-stage talent and ultimately began angel investing independently in defiance of those norms.
