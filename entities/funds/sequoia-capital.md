### 2026-05-09 — Fund XII benchmarking analysis

Joe analysed Sequoia Capital Fund XII (2006 vintage, $439.5M, US venture only) as a benchmark for [[Haven Ventures]] Fund One. Sequoia XII made 32 investments over approximately five years, deployed 65% of capital as lead, had a 15–18% write-off rate, and generated an estimated 8–13x gross MOIC. ### 2026-04-21 — Equity trap risk for founders
<!-- source_hash: fc2260c03740da7adcd2fce63d09bed8971b488aa3361c2e7b5a7bdac11c4d3c -->
Fraser argued that founders who raise from [[Sequoia]] at high valuations for platform roll-up plays are unlikely to get rich. Sequoia's liquidation preferences and return requirements — needing roughly 5x on their capital before founders see meaningful liquidity — trap the founder's equity inside the structure indefinitely, forcing continued acquisitions to satisfy growth demands. Fraser characterized this path as effectively making the founder a highly-paid employee with phantom equity rather than a genuine wealth-creation vehicle.
