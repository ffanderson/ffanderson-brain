### 2026-06-17 — Residency grant and equity clause
<!-- source_hash: baf1719e1d252f857bf066b3a18c980ff5b8361facf4fbe0fec40b05a1de8a56 -->
Neo provided a $40,000 grant per person for the residency with no upfront equity. However, the contract includes a clause that if a resident commercialises the project and incorporates, Neo holds the option — but not the obligation — to take up to 5% equity at its accelerator terms of $750,000 for 5%. Fraser noted this was not how the program had been marketed to him and flagged it as a potential source of friction.

### 2026-06-17 — Model for coding challenges and fellowship

### 2026-06-17 — Investment terms structure
<!-- source_hash: 274cc2fb4c3838d76e948808aee0f38b289099bd7632232db66e2813adf7eaab -->
Neo's fellowship terms were described as a $40k summer stipend to founders, which serves as the purchase price for an option to invest $750k for 5% equity plus an additional $750k uncapped upon commercialization. The $40k is treated as consumed regardless of whether the option is exercised, and there is reportedly a proviso preventing Neo from being completely diluted out in a low-valuation safe-stacking scenario. These terms are published on Neo's website.
