### 2026-05-07 — Core technology: GPU kernel optimization
<!-- source_hash: dced6a7a7fb7e7a1dfd861c88845d48d723078feee2653bd858416b6274687f4 -->
Standard Kernel's engine converts GPU assembly from across the fragmented GPU programming language landscape into a unified, LLM-readable representation, then uses that representation to recombine techniques from disparate kernels into optimized outputs. The system can observe live executing kernels, retrieve their code, and generate one-to-one replacements with zero runtime overhead; many-to-one replacements are possible but introduce overhead requiring careful management. End-to-end edits combining high- and low-level changes yield the largest gains, with reported 2x–4x improvements on RNN and LSTM workloads produced in collaboration with [[Jump Trading]].
↳ source: [[2026-05-07-intro-call-chris-standard-kernel-and-josh-beacon-nadia]]
