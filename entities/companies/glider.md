# Glider

## Mentions


### 2026-05-07 — Platform overview and positioning
<!-- source_hash: 974b4ac289d5d2ace9d857a653640b4c1355c28416cce724f24efd962c93a7b9 -->
Glider is a workflow and engagement platform built on deep context engineering and hundreds of dynamically updated prompts, designed to accelerate startup validation by acting as an AI co-founder, mentor, and coach. Jim positioned it as 'modeling excellence' versus trial-and-error, claiming teams using it validate more ideas faster, waste fewer resources on weak hypotheses, and accumulate institutional memory. The platform tracks experiments, generates interview guides, performs sentiment analysis on recorded interviews, and scores teams weekly on an Investment Readiness Level analogous to NASA's Technology Readiness Level.

### 2026-05-04 — Platform architecture and workflow
<!-- source_hash: cdd2e6c9b4769f8e6f7cdfbed296017bfef71a39b5344fbdf8731bd217b71ae8 -->
Glider is structured around cards, connections, and comments, with an evidence vault that aggregates documents, web clippings via a Chrome extension, and meeting recordings. AI-generated content is displayed in blue; content co-validated by humans shifts to blue-and-white, signaling higher confidence. The platform includes Zoom integration for sentiment analysis designed to counteract founder 'happy ears' bias, and an Investment Readiness Level scoring system that gives teams a quantifiable, weekly progress metric with specific improvement actions.
