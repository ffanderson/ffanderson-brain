### 2026-04-21 — Fund structure and LP economics
<!-- source_hash: 792c8fee85fbe6c2dc36471e01a7d2540f319d7064e43aad8119f1494f6f0ffb -->
Fraser advised that Gideon should be structured as a traditional GP/LP venture fund rather than a novel annual-vintage model, at least for the first vehicle, to make fundraising more straightforward. He sketched a simple economics model for a $20M fund: roughly $500k in total management fees, 25% carry, and 20–25% reserves earmarked for follow-on into winners, leaving approximately $14.5M for primary investments. Fraser noted that a 0.5-and-25 fee structure would be highly compelling to LPs compared with the standard 2-and-20, and the math should be made explicit in materials.
