### 2026-06-17 — Open-source/hosted model as product inspiration
<!-- source_hash: 46df11c7a62056b4ad545895142d97dbf46dd94d26a05d3b191c99dd4d57ab02 -->
Fraser mentioned Obsidian as a model worth emulating for a personal memory product: it is open-source for self-hosters but charges for cloud hosting, which he argued reduces the barrier for technically sophisticated users to trust the system with their data while still creating a monetization path. He noted Obsidian is experiencing renewed interest due to Claude integrations and its graph-based approach to surfacing connections between notes.
