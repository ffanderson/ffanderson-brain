### 2026-05-07 — First venture capital firm, origin story
<!-- source_hash: c1cbf444f00990cabbd78769085e004dcb463b48400c2320903980ec92011096 -->
American Research Development Corporation was described as the first institutional venture capital firm, founded by Georges Doriot — a French immigrant who served as quartermaster general for U.S. Army procurement during World War II — in partnership with MIT president Carl Compton. ARDC was associated with the 12 Lime Street house (later home of Vannevar Bush) and is credited with backing [[Digital]] and, notably, with passing on AltaVista, which the host described as a missed opportunity that preceded Google.
