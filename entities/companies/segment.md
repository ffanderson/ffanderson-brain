Fraser cited Segment as a canonical example of his investment thesis: three roommates living in Kendall Square (near Zog Pizza) who could not get adoption for their analytics product, got into Y Combinator as a last resort, and pivoted to a middleware pixel-router that became the standard event-routing layer between analytics tools like Mixpanel. Fraser noted the deal came in at a valuation around $3.2M and called it a prime illustration of smart, bonded founders stumbling into the right idea — 'the shittier the idea, the better for us.'
