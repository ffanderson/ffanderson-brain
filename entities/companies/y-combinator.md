### 2026-05-04 — Batch model critique
<!-- source_hash: cdd2e6c9b4769f8e6f7cdfbed296017bfef71a39b5344fbdf8731bd217b71ae8 -->
Jim critiqued Y Combinator's demo-day model, arguing that measuring success by funding rates and celebrating unicorns obscures the program's high portfolio mortality rate. He noted that Paul Graham's original insight rested on two structural cost eliminations — AWS removing the need for in-house hardware and Google AdSense reducing the need for a direct sales force — and that those advantages have since been widely commoditized. Jim contrasted Y Combinator's approach with Glider's emphasis on lessons-learned over pitch-day theater.
