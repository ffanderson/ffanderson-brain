# 3DX Thermal

## Mentions

### 2026-04-24 — Technology overview and differentiation
<!-- source_hash: fe182c655de62fb007b93a7fea9ea2813ee78c8e07d640b8a9f0185d11dff9b5 -->
3DX Thermal is commercializing a patented passive, capillary-driven two-phase cooling technology originating from University of Virginia research (~2014, DOE grants), with IP development ongoing since 2019. The system evaporates pure water at the chip surface and transports heat in the vapor phase without pumps, separating liquid and vapor flows entirely. This avoids the nucleate boiling superheat penalty (5–15°C) and mixed-phase flow instability that afflict pumped two-phase competitors like [[Celsius]] and [[Zutacore]].
