### 2026-05-05 — Preference stack construction and J.P. Morgan pilot
<!-- source_hash: bf186fec98e05ae092887c8aa1265515558a1f23b8a666aa8858f2f1350813b5 -->
Joe described Hamza as the clearest example of his asymmetric-information reserve rule: when Hamza signed a pilot with J.P. Morgan — a deal Joe said he personally brokered — he knew the company's fundamental value had moved materially before the market reflected it. He responded by brokering a deal to buy out the common tranche at a 50% discount to the Series A price and subsequently offered structured call options to senior executives taking pay cuts, giving them the right to sell up to $1M of equity at the current price per share if they hit defined OKRs. Joe also noted he and Dana (whom he identified as a co-lead from [[Greycroft]]) hold equal preference seniority with no investor above them, and that Joe holds a common tranche Dana does not.
