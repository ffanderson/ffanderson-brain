### 2026-05-05 — Category creation and SAP exit

### 2026-04-21 — Pivots, churn, and eventual SAP exit
<!-- source_hash: c40ccca14cd227462623da16ee9371f25ed8049bf6433949ce1d7119e25ed339 -->
Gigya pivoted twice — first around $10M ARR due to a market change, and again around $30M ARR where churn was the underlying issue. Patrick noted that churn is masked at low ARR by growth velocity but becomes structurally damaging once the business approaches $20M ARR. After redeveloping the product, a new business line scaled from zero to approximately $100M run rate over four to five years, ultimately leading to an acquisition by [[SAP]].
