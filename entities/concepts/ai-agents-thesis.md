# AI Agents Thesis

## Definition

AI agents are autonomous systems that can plan, reason, use tools, and complete
multi-step tasks with minimal human intervention. Unlike chatbots (single-turn Q&A)
or copilots (human-in-the-loop assistance), agents operate independently toward goals.

## Why It Matters

1. **Labor leverage**: Agents can multiply knowledge worker output 10-100x for routine tasks
2. **New software paradigm**: Shift from "tools humans use" to "agents that use tools"
3. **Enterprise value**: High-value workflows are automatable for first time
4. **Timing**: Foundation models now capable enough; tooling/infra emerging

## Key Beliefs

- Vertical agents (domain-specific) will win before horizontal platforms
- Enterprise workflows have higher willingness-to-pay than consumer
- Trust and reliability are the core product challenges
- Founder-market fit matters enormously in this space

## Investment Criteria

Looking for:
- Technical founders with AI/ML depth
- Specific vertical or workflow focus
- Enterprise GTM (not consumer)
- Evidence of working agent (not just chatbot)
- Understanding of failure modes and edge cases
